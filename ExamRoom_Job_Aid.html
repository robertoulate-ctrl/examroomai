Decision Framework

Choose the right assessment tool for your situation

👨‍💼 Role Type: Technical Positions → Use ExamRoom.AI

Why? Technical assessments require objective, measurable criteria. ExamRoom.AI's automated scoring removes bias and ensures consistency.

Examples: Software engineers, data analysts, systems administrators, database administrators, QA engineers.

Assessment Elements: Code reviews, problem-solving scenarios, technical knowledge tests, debugging exercises.

🗣️ Role Type: Communication-Heavy → Use Spark Hire

Why? Soft skills require subjective evaluation. Spark Hire's video interviews allow assessment of communication, professionalism, and presentation skills.

Examples: Sales representatives, customer service, account managers, recruiters, public relations.

Assessment Elements: One-way video questions, behavioral responses, communication clarity, personality fit.

⚖️ High Volume Screening → Use ExamRoom.AI

Why? When screening hundreds of candidates, automated assessment saves time and ensures uniform evaluation.

Efficiency Gain: 80-90% faster screening compared to manual review.

Ideal For: University recruiting, entry-level positions, contractor networks, global hiring campaigns.

🎯 Compliance-Critical Roles → Use ExamRoom.AI

Why? ExamRoom.AI provides detailed audit trails, ensuring EEOC and adverse impact compliance.

Documentation: Every assessment is scored consistently with full reporting capabilities.

Industries: Healthcare, finance, legal, utilities, government contracts.

💡 Quick Evaluation Needed → Use Spark Hire

Why? Spark Hire is faster to deploy and simpler to evaluate than technical assessments.

Timeline: Setup in hours, results in 24-48 hours.

Use Case: Urgent hiring, seasonal roles, replacement hiring, contractor assessment.

⚠️ Important Consideration: The best approach often uses BOTH platforms in sequence. Use ExamRoom.AI first for technical screening, then Spark Hire for finalists to assess cultural fit and soft skills.

Recruitment Workflow

Step-by-step process for implementing assessments

📋 Phase 1: Preparation & Planning
1

Define Assessment Needs

Identify which role competencies need assessment. Document required skills, experience levels, and job-critical knowledge areas.

2

Select Platform(s)

Using the decision framework above, choose ExamRoom.AI, Spark Hire, or both for your assessment strategy.

3

Configure Test Parameters

Set assessment difficulty, time limits, question counts, and passing scores. Align with role requirements and candidate pool expectations.

4

Create Scoring Rubric

Document what constitutes pass/fail, develop evaluation criteria, and calibrate with your team.

📤 Phase 2: Distribution & Administration
1

Send Assessments

Invite candidates through your ATS integration or direct email. Provide clear instructions and set realistic completion deadlines (typically 3-7 days).

2

Monitor Participation

Track completion rates daily. Send reminders to non-completers and offer technical support if needed.

3

Proctor for Security

For high-security roles, ensure proper test environment, identification verification, and anti-cheating measures.

4

Manage Accommodations

Provide extended time for ADA accommodations. Document all modifications for compliance and audit trail.

📊 Phase 3: Evaluation & Analysis
1

Review Automated Scores

ExamRoom.AI provides immediate scoring. Review reports, check for outliers, and identify top performers.

2

Conduct Manual Review

For Spark Hire or borderline candidates, perform detailed video review and scoring against your rubric.

3

Check Adverse Impact

Analyze results by demographic group. Document any disparate impact and have business justification ready for compliance.

4

Create Pass/Fail Groups

Identify top performers (advance to interview), borderline (consider re-assessment), and non-qualified (respectfully decline).

🎤 Phase 4: Interview & Selection
1

Prepare Interview Questions

Use assessment results to guide interview focus. Ask deeper questions about assessed competencies.

2

Conduct Interviews

Score consistently using structured interviews. Reference assessment performance to explore strengths and gaps.

3

Reference Checks

Verify assessment results through reference calls. Ask about job-critical competencies identified in screening.

4

Make Offer Decision

Combine assessment, interview, and reference scores. Document decision rationale for future compliance audit.

📈 Phase 5: Measurement & Optimization
1

Track Hire Performance

At 90 days, 6 months, and 1 year, measure hired candidate performance against their assessment scores.

2

Calculate Validity

Determine correlation between assessment scores and on-the-job performance. Adjust cutoff scores if needed.

3

Audit for Bias

Annually review adverse impact data. Ensure assessment remains job-related and predictive for all groups.

4

Optimize & Iterate

Update assessments quarterly based on new role requirements, market changes, and performance data.

Best Practices & Compliance

Ensure fairness, compliance, and effectiveness

Assessment Design Principles

✅ Job-Related & Valid

Assessments must measure competencies truly required for the job. Document the job analysis supporting your assessment design. Use industry research and SME input to validate question relevance.

✅ Standardized Administration

All candidates receive identical questions, time limits, and instructions. This consistency is legally required under FCRA and EEOC guidelines and improves statistical validity.

✅ Clear Scoring Standards

Create objective scoring rubrics. Train evaluators to score consistently. For Spark Hire, score all videos using identical criteria to reduce subjective bias.

✅ Transparent Communication

Tell candidates upfront that assessments are part of your hiring process. Explain what's being assessed and why. Provide score feedback to unsuccessful candidates upon request.

Compliance Requirements

⚖️ FCRA Compliance

ExamRoom.AI and Spark Hire assessments may be considered "background checks" under the Fair Credit Reporting Act. Obtain signed written consent before administering. Provide FCRA-compliant disclosure forms. Have adverse action procedures ready.

⚖️ EEOC Adverse Impact

Track pass rates by race/ethnicity, gender, age, disability. If any group has a pass rate below 80% of the highest group, you may have adverse impact. Document business necessity and job-relatedness. Consider alternative assessments if disparate impact exists.

⚖️ ADA Accommodations

Provide extended time for candidates with disabilities (typically 50% extra time). Offer alternative formats (large print, screen reader compatible, etc.). Keep detailed accommodation records. Never penalize for requesting accommodation.

⚖️ GDPR/Privacy Compliance

For European candidates, ensure GDPR compliance. Get explicit consent for data processing. Retain assessment data only as long as necessary (typically 1-3 years). Provide candidates access to their assessment data on request.

Adverse Impact Prevention

Risk Factor Prevention Strategy
Language-Based Bias Use clear, simple language unless language proficiency is job-required. Avoid industry jargon unless essential. Test with diverse proofreaders.
Cultural Assumptions Avoid culture-specific references or examples. Use scenarios that are universally understandable. Review with diverse content team.
Difficulty Calibration Pilot test with candidate pools to ensure difficulty aligns with actual job requirements. Adjust if certain groups consistently underperform.
Time Pressure Allow adequate time for completion. Rushed assessments may disadvantage certain populations. Typical guideline: 1.5 minutes per question minimum.
Technology Barriers Test across devices and internet connections. Provide tech support phone line. Offer alternative assessment methods if tech is not job-critical.

Accommodations & Accessibility

🦾 Disability Accommodations

Extended Time: 50% additional time standard; up to 100% for severe disabilities. Screen Reader Compatible: Ensure Spark Hire videos have closed captions. Provide transcripts. Large Print: ExamRoom.AI supports 150-200% zoom. Separate Room: Allow test-takers to use quiet environment with monitor. Scribe/Reader: Provide if writing or reading is not job-critical.

👨‍🦯 Reasonable Accommodations Process

1. Request: Candidate requests accommodation in writing (email acceptable). 2. Analyze: Determine if job-related and show business necessity. 3. Implement: Provide accommodation without penalty. 4. Document: Keep detailed records of request, analysis, and accommodation provided. 5. Evaluate: Test accommodation to ensure it works for candidate.

Data Security & Privacy

🔒 Candidate Data Protection

Assessments contain sensitive data: performance history, behavioral responses, video recordings. Limit access to HR and hiring managers only. Use password-protected accounts. Enable two-factor authentication. Encrypt data in transit and at rest. Conduct regular access audits.

🔒 Retention Policies

Define how long to retain assessment data. Typical: hired employees (2-3 years), non-hired (1 year), legal disputes (7 years). Delete data securely beyond retention period. Document deletion. Some states require 1-year minimum retention.

Documentation & Audit Trail

📋 Record Keeping Best Practices

Keep: Job descriptions supporting assessment, job analyses, pilot test results, scoring rubrics, adverse impact analyses, accommodation requests and outcomes, training records for scorers, validation studies. This documentation defends against discrimination claims and supports legal compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Practical guidance for common scenarios

Can I use these assessments in multiple countries? +
Yes, but with important caveats. US laws (FCRA, EEOC, ADA) apply if candidates are US-based. European candidates trigger GDPR requirements. Canada requires provincial compliance. For international hiring, consult legal counsel about data residency, privacy, and employment laws in each jurisdiction. ExamRoom.AI and Spark Hire support multiple languages, but content must still comply with local regulations.
What if a candidate scores low but has strong past experience? +
Treat this as valuable interview fodder. Ask specifically about the low-scoring areas. Possible reasons: test anxiety, bad day, assessment doesn't capture their actual capability, or they genuinely lack this specific skill. Don't automatically reject based on scores alone. Use assessment as data point alongside resume and references. If you advance borderline candidates to interviews, ensure you're doing this consistently, not sporadically, to maintain legally defensible process.
How do I handle technical issues during testing? +
Have a support process ready: (1) Post testing window help desk available, (2) If candidate reports mid-test: allow restart with fresh test (don't count partial attempt), (3) Document all issues for audit trail, (4) Be consistent—if one candidate gets restart, others with similar issues should too, (5) Consider backup assessment methods for critical technical failures, (6) Communicate clearly with candidate about issue resolution.
What score should I use as the passing threshold? +
This depends on: (1) Job criticality—safety-sensitive roles need higher passing scores, (2) Candidate pool quality—strong pool may support higher cutoff, (3) Adverse impact—if high cutoff creates disparate impact, lower it if possible while maintaining job-relatedness, (4) Validation data—ideally, you've studied correlation between scores and job performance to set evidence-based cutoff, (5) Business needs—fewer false negatives (miss good candidates) vs. false positives (hire weak candidates).
How transparent should I be about assessment criteria? +
More transparency is generally better. Tell candidates: (1) What competencies are being assessed and why they're job-critical, (2) How the assessment works (time limit, question count, format), (3) How you'll use results (part of hiring decision), (4) Timeline for feedback, (5) That they can request accommodation, (6) What happens if they don't pass (typically, they're not advanced to interview). FCRA and best practice both recommend this transparency. It improves candidate experience and reduces legal risk.
Can I use assessment data to make conclusions about culture fit? +
Carefully. ExamRoom.AI behavioral components and Spark Hire video responses can provide culture fit signals, but don't rely on them as determinants. Culture fit assessment is inherently subjective and carries discrimination risk—what you perceive as "bad culture fit" may actually be protected class differences. Use assessments for job-critical competencies only. Make culture fit decisions through interviews with trained, consistent interviewers using structured questions. Document that culture fit preferences are based on genuine business needs, not protected characteristics.
How often should I update my assessments? +
At minimum annually, but preferably quarterly for fast-changing roles. Update when: (1) Job description changes significantly, (2) New technologies or tools become job-critical, (3) You identify adverse impact issues, (4) Hired employees' performance data shows assessment wasn't predictive, (5) Industry standards shift. Keep version control of assessments. Document changes for audit trail. Whenever you update, consider piloting with small candidate pool before full deployment.
What should I do if I discover adverse impact in my assessment data? +
(1) Don't panic—this is discoverable in EEOC investigations anyway. (2) Document your analysis thoroughly showing the disparate impact. (3) Conduct job analysis to justify business necessity. (4) If assessment is not job-critical, consider removing or modifying it. (5) If it's job-critical but still shows disparate impact, investigate why: cultural bias in questions, language barrier, testing conditions, time pressure, etc. (6) Pilot alternative assessment methods. (7) If modifying, track results for next 6-12 months to ensure change reduced disparate impact. (8) Keep all documentation for compliance defense.
Can I share assessment results with hiring managers? +
Yes—hiring managers need assessment results to make hiring decisions. However: (1) Only share with those directly involved in hiring decision, (2) Educate managers on how to interpret results (correlation is not causation), (3) Warn against bias—assessment is data point, not destiny, (4) Don't include personally identifiable information beyond what's needed, (5) Maintain confidentiality—don't share results with other candidates or team members not involved in hiring, (6) Under GDPR, candidates can request their results, so maintain ability to provide this.
What's the difference between using assessment as a screening tool vs. validation? +
Screening: You use assessment results to decide who moves forward (pass/fail gate). Lighter compliance burden but less data-driven. Validation: You use assessment results to predict job performance, studying correlation between scores and actual job performance. Heavier documentation burden but stronger legal defense. Best practice: Start with screening based on job analysis and industry benchmarks. After 6-12 months of hiring data, validate your cutoff scores. If they don't predict performance, adjust. This iterative validation process builds increasingly defensible assessments.

📝 Purpose

Startup Questions are essential configuration parameters that define how your organization will use ExamRoom.AI, including platform settings, proctoring rules, and exam administration procedures.

⚙️ Scope

Covers 56+ key questions organized into 6 categories, plus 31 additional exam-specific configurations.

Complete the Excel file, convert to JSON, validate, and upload to ExamRoom.AI for immediate implementation.

Category Items Description
Organization Info 3 Organization name, payment model, candidate ID creation preferences
Contact Info 2 Support contacts and incident response procedures
Platform Config 3 Assessment platform type, console URLs, exam URLs
Exam Settings 2 Timer behavior and answer persistence on disconnection
Proctoring Config 2 Access credentials and technical support procedures
Other Settings 44 Eligibility, check-in, identification, registration, technology requirements

Step-by-Step Completion Guide

📋 Step 1: Gather Information
1

Collect Organizational Details

Gather company name, branding preferences, and decision-maker contact information for ExamRoom support.

2

Document Platform Details

Note your current assessment platform type, console URL, and any existing exam URLs or system integrations.

3

Define Testing Rules

Determine your organization's testing policies: timer behavior, answer saving, reconnection rules, and eligibility requirements.

✍️ Step 2: Complete the Excel File
1

Open Startup Questions.xlsx

Open the Excel file in your spreadsheet application. Review the "EX.AI Startup Questions" sheet.

2

Fill in Answers

For each question in column A, provide your organization's answer in column C. Add any relevant comments in column D.

3

Complete Additional Information

Review the "Additional Exams Info" sheet and complete any exam-specific configurations relevant to your use case.

4

Review for Completeness

Verify all required questions are answered. Consult with stakeholders on critical decisions (payment model, proctoring requirements, etc).

Verify Output

3

Review JSON Structure

Open the JSON file in your editor to verify all data is correctly captured and structured.

✅ Step 4: Validate & Deploy
1

Internal Review

Have your HR/Recruiting team review the configuration for accuracy and completeness.

2

Stakeholder Approval

Get sign-off from key stakeholders on critical configurations (proctoring, eligibility, etc).

3

Upload to ExamRoom.AI

Submit the JSON file to ExamRoom.AI through their API or admin dashboard for implementation.

4

Monitor & Adjust

After implementation, monitor for any issues and adjust configurations as needed.

2. Payment Model: Do candidates or your organization pay for proctoring?

3. Candidate ID: Will ExamRoom create IDs or does your organization prefer to provide them?

📱 Platform Config - Key Questions

1. Assessment Platform: What is your current test platform?

2. Console URL: What is your test console URL?

3. Exam URLs: What are the URLs for your exams?

⚙️ Exam Settings - Key Questions

1. Timer Behavior: Should the timer stop if a candidate disconnects?

2. Answer Persistence: Should answers be saved if the candidate disconnects?

👮 Proctoring Config - Key Questions

1. Access Credentials: Will proctors need credentials to provide test access?

2. Support Procedures: How should proctors respond to technical issues?

📋 Other Settings - Key Questions

Eligibility: Is eligibility required before scheduling? Will it come from a database or LMS?

Check-in: What identification methods will you use? (Government ID, badges, etc.)

Technology: Do candidates need the ExamRoom 360 app? Browser extension?

Registration: Will candidates create accounts or will you provide information?

Best Practices for Startup Configuration

✅ Involve Stakeholders

Include HR, Recruiting, IT, Compliance, and Finance in configuration decisions. This ensures all organizational needs are addressed.

✅ Start Simple

Begin with core configurations (organization info, basic exam settings). You can add complexity later as you become familiar with ExamRoom.

✅ Document Decisions

Record why you made each choice in the Comments column. This helps during audits and when onboarding new team members.

✅ Test Before Full Rollout

Run a pilot test with small candidate group using your configuration. Gather feedback and adjust before full implementation.

✅ Version Control

Keep versions of your Excel and JSON files. This allows you to track changes and rollback if needed.

Common Configuration Scenarios

Scenario Key Configurations
Academic Institution Eligibility from student database, room scan required, proctored remote exams, government ID verification
Corporate Hiring Candidate-created accounts, remote proctoring optional, quick check-in process, timer continues on disconnect
Certification Program Pre-registration required, photo ID verification, proctored exams, answers saved on disconnect, database eligibility
Unproctored Assessments Minimal check-in, no proctoring required, no ID verification, self-paced, answers auto-saved
✅ Pro Tip: After your initial configuration is live, set a 30-day check-in to review how the settings are working. Gather feedback from proctors, candidates, and administrators to identify any needed adjustments.
